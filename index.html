01 / Executive Summary

The proposal is organized around how the work behaves.

USDA should not have to reverse-engineer a blended rate. Each module below names what is included, the operating threshold that applies, and the charge that activates when an event falls outside that threshold. The network is broad enough to expand without re-procurement.

Warehousing readiness
$220/ MT

Reserved food-grade operating capacity with defined dwell, density, surge, and container thresholds.

Transportation
$1,200/ move

Standard container or truck movement; chassis, overweight, demurrage, detention, and terminal storage are separately visible.

Loadout execution
$46/ MT

Export staging, stuffing, sequencing, and vessel coordination within defined operating thresholds.

Ancillary support
$9/ MT

Routine reporting, inspection coordination, compliance support, and exception management.

What USDA avoids

  • Ambiguous all-in rate with unstated assumptions
  • Unlimited readiness expectation absorbed into a single price
  • Unclear treatment of delays, containers, reefer, or surge
  • Line-item confusion when operations change

What NAVCOR delivers

  • Defined base rate by module
  • Measurable included thresholds for each module
  • Formula-based charges when thresholds are exceeded
  • Single point of accountability across the network
Commercial structure

$3,208,333 — every month, all four locations.

Prime structureNAVCOR operates as USDA's single point of contract and accountability, delivering the program through established U.S. and international logistics partners under a unified commercial framework.

03 / Transportation

Transportation is priced by movement.

Port transportation is driven by container moves, fuel, chassis days, cargo weight, and delay exposure. Pricing standard movement at $1,200 keeps USDA's transportation visibility tied to actual port activity, with chassis, overweight, demurrage, detention, and terminal storage handled as transparent pass-through.

Base movement
$1,200/ container move

Standard port-market container or truck movement, including routine port coordination, drayage, scheduling, and movement risk under normal operating conditions.

What is included

  • Standard local drayage within the associated port market
  • Routine container or truck positioning
  • Routine port coordination and scheduling
  • Standard export staging movement under normal operating conditions

06 / Annual Readiness

One flat monthly fee. All four locations. All in.

USDA pays a single flat monthly fee of $3,208,333 covering full readiness across all four locations (140,000 MT total). It is paid every month, regardless of activity level. Container transportation, threshold triggers, and pass-throughs are billed as separate add-ons when they actually occur.

The model is simple because the thresholds are clear.

Fixed monthly readiness for the core operating environment, movement-based transportation for actual port activity, and formula-based charges only when USDA's operating pattern exceeds defined thresholds.

What is normal

Covered by base rates and defined operating thresholds across all four modules.

What is extraordinary

Triggered by measurable events — dwell, surge, reefer, empty accumulation, weekend loadout, special inspection.

Why it works

Revenue tracks operational burden. USDA sees clear pricing; the program remains executable across the full network.
